ANALYSIS RULES:
1. MODE ADAPTATION:
   - "joined": Shipment + carrier + route data combined is the primary truth.
   - "both": Start network-level (joined), then drill into carrier/route nuances.
   - "separate": Find patterns across warehouses, carriers, or delivery zones.

2. DATA INTEGRITY:
   - NO IDs: Never reference shipment IDs, tracking IDs, or order IDs as dimensions.
   - DISCRETE: Treat carrier codes, route types, priority levels, and status codes as categorical.
   - SIGNIFICANCE: Correlations < |0.10| = "Noise". Do not report as operational trends.
   - QUALITY: Apply SEMANTIC NULLS PROTOCOL (rule 6) — distinguish missing scan events (technical gaps) from sparse exception columns.
   - CAUSATION: Use "associated with" not "caused" unless correlation > 0.6.

3. DATA HONESTY: No speculation. If corr <0.1, state "inconclusive".
   For mandatory field nulls >5% (e.g., Carrier, Origin, Destination, Ship Date), you MUST add a "Data Limitations" subsection. Missing scan events fall into this category.
   For sparse exception columns (e.g., Damage Report, SLA Breach, Insurance Claim), analyze the non-null "Exception Cohort" — do NOT add a limitations subsection.

4. STRUCTURE:
   - Link carrier performance to route economics and delivery SLA compliance.
   - Directly address CROSS-SHEET HYPOTHESIS in conclusion.
   - Explicitly flag data that contradicts the hypothesis.

5. OUTPUT FORMAT:
   - Use professional Markdown.
   - Every claim MUST cite a specific metric from the JSON.

6. SEMANTIC NULLS PROTOCOL — Apply BEFORE any null-rate decision:
   CRITICAL: Do not treat all nulls as data failures. Classify every high-null column:
   a. TECHNICAL GAPS: Missing mandatory fields (e.g., Carrier, Origin, Destination, Ship Date). If >5% null → report as an operational data gap. Missing scan events are the most common example.
   b. MEANINGFUL SPARSITY: Columns representing rare operational events (e.g., Damage Report, Insurance Claim, Exception Event, SLA Breach, Recall). Nulls = valid "No Exception" state.
   Rule: For sparse exception columns, treat null as baseline (no exception occurred). Analyze non-null rows as the "Exception Cohort". Do NOT add a "Data Limitations" subsection for these columns.

GOLD STANDARD LOGISTICS FRAMEWORKS — Apply ALL three + two logistics-specific lenses:

A. PARETO CONCENTRATION (80/20 Rule):
   - Which 20% of routes, carriers, or origin-destination pairs drive 80% of cost, volume, or delays?
   - Identify the "critical arteries" of the network vs. the long-tail routes.
   - Flag: if a single carrier handles >40% of volume, that is a single-point-of-failure risk.

B. BIMODAL DISTRIBUTION — Express vs. Standard Segmentation:
   - Are transit times or costs bimodal? (Express lanes vs. economy lanes?)
   - Is on-time delivery rate bimodal across carrier types or regions?
   - Report both cluster means, volume share, and cost share.
   - NEVER average express and standard shipments — they represent fundamentally different SLA contracts.

C. PARADOX DETECTION — Counter-Intuitive Logistics Findings:
   - Tag confirmed paradoxes with [PARADOX].
   - Classic logistics paradoxes: premium carrier → higher damage rate; shorter route → higher cost per km;
     most frequent carrier → worst on-time performance; largest warehouse → highest stockout rate.
     (The "Shipper Paradox": the preferred carrier in terms of volume is not the best in terms of SLA.)

D. LOGISTICS-SPECIFIC: SLA Compliance & Delay Root Cause:
   - Segment on-time delivery rate by carrier, route type, and distance tier.
   - Identify the carriers and routes with the widest gap between contracted SLA and actual performance.
   - Flag any systemic delays correlated with specific origin zones or weather-prone corridors.

E. LOGISTICS-SPECIFIC: Cost Efficiency vs. Service Level Trade-off:
   - Correlate cost per shipment with delivery time and damage rate simultaneously.
   - Identify the "efficiency frontier" carriers: best service level per unit cost.
   - Flag carriers that are expensive AND unreliable (negative value proposition).
   - Identify underutilized high-performing carriers (low volume, high SLA compliance).
