=== SELF-VALIDATION CHECKLIST ===

1. CORRELATION THRESHOLD
   Any correlation with |r| < 0.10 must be labeled "inconclusive (noise-level signal)"
   or removed entirely. Do not present noise as a finding.

2. SAMPLE SIZE FLAGS
   Any insight derived from fewer than 30 data points must include the caveat:
   "(limited sample — treat as indicative only)".

3. TAUTOLOGY SCAN
   Remove or rephrase any insight that is definitionally true or a physical/biological constant:
   - "Revenue increases when Price and Quantity both increase" → tautology, remove.
   - "Height correlates with Weight" → biological baseline, remove.
   - "Older athletes have competed longer" → definitional, remove.
   Replace with the anomaly, deviation, or specific magnitude the data actually reveals.

4. BUSINESS IMPACT
   Every insight must end with a concrete business action or decision implication.
   "Investigate further" is not acceptable unless you specify: what to investigate,
   what decision depends on the answer, and which team should act.

5. NOVELTY JUSTIFICATION
   For each insight, ask: "Would a domain expert assume this without seeing our specific data?"
   If yes, add what makes this data's finding non-obvious — unexpected magnitude, specific
   customer segment, time-period anomaly, or exception to the general rule.

6. SPARSE EVENT MISCLASSIFICATION
   Scan every finding that mentions a high null rate or "missing data".
   For each one, ask: is this column a Sparse Event?
   A Sparse Event column records a rare or exception-like occurrence where null
   simply means "the event did not happen" (e.g., Medals, Defaults, Fraud flags,
   Returns, Bonuses, Injuries, Promotions).

   If the column IS a Sparse Event and the report treats its null rate as a
   data quality issue or limitation, flag the finding as:
     "Logical Error: Trivializing a sparse event — high null rate is expected.
      Reframe as Target Cohort Analysis: focus on the N% of rows where the
      event occurred, not on the absence of the event."

   Then rewrite the offending finding using the correct framing:
   "X% of [entities] experienced [event] — this cohort shows..."
